Taiji boxing, also known as thirteen movements, includes quivering, smoothing, squeezing, pressing, picking, pushing, elbowing, leaning, advancing, retreating, walking left, walking right and halting. In terms of the Eight Diagrams, the first four movements are concerned with the four directions of north, south, east and west, while the middle four movements with the four directions of northwest, southwest, northeast and southeast. In therms of the Five Elements, the last five movements match metal, wood, water, fire and earth respectively. Since each movement is closely related with Yin and Yang, the thirteen movements are also termed Taiji Boxing.
